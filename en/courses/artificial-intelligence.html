Artificial Intelligence

We are teaching secondary school students the AI Age course.

Time: Thursday, 7:00pm-8:30pm
Location: One-North Launchpad Block #67

Step into the AI Age with a practical introduction to modern artificial intelligence, its applications, and the people, tools, and ethics shaping the future. Our focus is on establishing a comprehensive and deep understanding of the AI Age and cultivating the core skills in the AI Age, especially critical, creative and compassionate thinking.

Coming Up

Lesson 3: Classical AI vs Machine Learning

We will learning a brief history of AI and get some hands-on experience of "old-fashioned" AI techniques.

Syllabus

  1. The AI Age Has Arrived -July 16

    AI in everyday life, education, industry, academia, government, and military, etc.; what is AI?

  2. Chatting with AI -July 23

    Basic applications: querying information and learning knowledge; prompting basics.

  3. Classical AI vs Machine Learning -July 30

    Two different approaches to AI: a historical perspective.

  4. How Machines Learn -August 6

    Data, neural networks, and training.

  5. Ethics and Risks -August 13

    Fairness, copyright, privacy, and harmful content.

  6. Python for AI -August 20

    Basic data structures, operations, control flow, and functions.

  7. Large Language Models and Generative AI -August 27

    Basic principles and characteristics.

  8. Beyond Chatting -September 3

    Advanced applications: writing, translating, coding, analysis, and problem solving.

  9. Building Your Own Mini-Agent -September 24

    AI workflows and autonomous task execution.

  10. Multimodal AI: Images and Speech -October 1

    AI that sees, paints, and speaks.

  11. AI in Education and Job Transformation -October 8

    New ways of learning and working.

  12. The Future of AI -October 29

    Economic revolution and philosophical debate.
